Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about booking and listing on RVSpot.

For Guests
Search for parks by location, dates, or amenities. When you find a park you like, select your arrival and departure dates, enter your rig details, and complete payment through our secure checkout. You'll receive a booking confirmation email immediately. The park operator is notified and will have your reservation ready on arrival.
RVSpot charges a 3% platform service fee on each booking to cover payment processing infrastructure and platform maintenance. This fee is shown transparently at checkout before you confirm. It is non-refundable except in cases where the park cancels your confirmed reservation.
Parks with a "Verified Pro" badge have been onboarded through our Park Pro program, which includes identity and business verification via Stripe Connect. All parks on RVSpot have been reviewed before listing. Community reviews from verified guests provide an additional layer of accountability — you can see honest ratings from real stays.
Yes, but the cancellation policy depends on the individual park. Each park sets its own policy (flexible, moderate, or strict), which is displayed on the booking page before you confirm. Common policies offer a full refund if cancelled 24–72 hours in advance. The 3% RVSpot platform fee is non-refundable in all cases. To cancel, go to your booking history in your account dashboard.
We accept all major credit and debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover) through Stripe's secure checkout. Apple Pay and Google Pay are supported on compatible devices and browsers. We do not accept cash, checks, or bank transfers at this time.
After your check-out date, you'll receive an email invitation to review your stay. You can also leave a review by visiting the park's page on RVSpot and clicking "Write a review." Reviews are only accepted from guests who have completed a verified booking at that park to ensure authenticity.
Nomad Pro is our $9/month subscription for full-time RV travelers. It unlocks offline maps and GPS routing, real Wi-Fi speed ratings (crowd-sourced from actual guests), park discounts averaging 15% off, access to the caravan organizer, and a remote job board for travelers who work on the road. Cancel anytime from your account dashboard.
Each park's profile page shows their phone number and, for Pro parks, a direct messaging option. For pre-booking questions about specific sites, rig accommodations, or accessibility needs, we recommend calling the park directly. Once you have a confirmed booking, you can also message the park through your booking dashboard.
For Park Operators
Your park may already be listed — we pre-populate listings from public sources. Search for your park on RVSpot and click "Claim this park" on the listing. We'll verify ownership via phone or business email, usually within 24 hours. Once claimed, you can update all details, add photos, and start accepting bookings. Visit our operators page to get started.
Listing your park is free. A Basic listing lets guests find you and book — we charge a 3% platform fee per booking. Park Pro is $69/month and removes all commission fees, so you keep 100% of each booking. Pro also includes priority search placement, an analytics dashboard, availability calendar, and verified Pro badge. See the full pricing page for a feature comparison.
Payments are processed via Stripe Connect. Once you connect your Stripe account (or create one during onboarding — it's free), payouts are deposited directly into your bank account 2–7 business days after the guest's check-in date. Stripe's standard card processing fees apply (typically 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction).
Stripe Connect is the payment infrastructure we use to route guest payments directly to park operators. When a guest pays for a booking, the funds (minus any applicable fees) flow directly to your connected Stripe account. You don't need a separate Stripe account beforehand — we guide you through creating one during the onboarding process. It's free and takes about 5 minutes.
Both plans let you accept bookings. The key differences: Basic charges a 3% fee per booking; Pro ($69/month) has zero commission. Pro also includes priority placement in search results, a booking analytics dashboard, availability calendar management, direct guest messaging, and a Verified Pro badge that increases guest trust. For most active parks, Pro pays for itself quickly by eliminating per-booking fees.
Yes — Park Pro operators have access to an availability calendar where you can block dates, set minimum stay requirements, and manage capacity. Basic listing operators currently cannot manage availability through the platform; guests contact the park directly to confirm availability before booking. Availability management is one of the key upgrades in the Pro plan.
After a guest's stay, they receive an automated invitation to leave a review. Reviews are verified — only guests with a completed booking at your park can leave one. You can respond publicly to reviews through your operator dashboard. We do not allow parks to remove reviews except in cases of clear policy violations (spam, offensive content, or reviews not based on a real stay).
From your operator dashboard, click "Upgrade to Pro" and complete the checkout. Your Pro features activate immediately. You'll also be guided through Stripe Connect setup if you haven't completed it yet. Park Pro renews monthly and can be cancelled at any time — your listing reverts to Basic at the end of the billing period with no data lost.
