FAQs for operators

Common questions

Do I need to use RVSpot's booking system, or can I keep my current system?+
You can use both. Free listings can direct inquiries to your existing website or phone. Pro Parks get RVSpot's native booking system with real-time availability, but it's not mandatory — you can sync via iCal and keep your existing system as the source of truth.
How does the 10% commission work for free listings?+
For free-tier parks, RVSpot processes bookings on your behalf and remits 90% of the nightly rate to your bank account within 5–7 business days after each stay. Pro Parks pay $0 in commissions — guests pay you directly through the booking system.
Can I control which dates are available and block off time?+
Yes. You have full control over availability. You can block individual dates, set seasonal closures, limit the booking window, and define minimum stay requirements per site. Pro Parks also get site-level availability management for granular control.
What happens to reviews I don't agree with?+
Pro Parks can publicly respond to any review, which is visible to all future visitors. Reviews can be flagged for policy violations (hate speech, fake reviews, etc.) and our team reviews within 48 hours. Reviews from verified stays (guests who booked through RVSpot) are labeled separately from unverified reviews.
Is there a contract or minimum commitment?+
No contracts. Pro Park is month-to-month. Cancel anytime before your next billing date. Annual plans are available at a 15% discount ($799/year vs $948/year billed monthly).
Can I list multiple parks under one account?+
Yes. One operator account can manage unlimited park listings. Each park is billed separately at $79/month if upgrading to Pro. Multi-park operators with 5+ parks should contact us for enterprise pricing.
